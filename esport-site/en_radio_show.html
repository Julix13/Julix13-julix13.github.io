The show “Here, It Plays” on Radio Sunshine welcomes Italian player Rachel Fiori, who came to France for training matches with the French team from Montpellier. She agreed to an interview to talk about the introduction of e-sports to the Olympics.

audio transcript

M: Hello, welcome to Radio Sunshine today in your new show "Ici ça joue," where we will welcome a player from the Italian League of Legends team, Rachel Fiori aka StormiNova.

J: Yes, because if you didn't know, e-sports will be part of the Olympic disciplines in 2028 in LA. And we will have a weekly show presenting an e-sports player.

M: After 6 years of negotiations with the International Olympic Committee, e-sports is finally recognized as an Olympic discipline, and fans will be able to follow the events on France TV and the live-streaming platform Twitch.

J: E-sports fans have reached out on our social media with a FAQ. We have selected some questions that we will ask Rachel.

M: I think we can welcome her among us, if you're okay with that, Juliette.

J: Yes, of course, Michael, and don’t forget, at the end there will be a game where you, dear listeners, can win two tickets for the 2028 Olympics in Los Angeles. Let’s welcome Rachel now.

Guest jingle

M + J: Hello Rachel!

R: Hello!

M: How is everything in Montpellier? Are you enjoying your time with us?

R: Yes, it's going very well, thank you.

J: You speak very, very good French!

R: Thank you, that’s kind. I come to France often, we do training with the French team in Montpellier.

M: So you're aware of e-sports entering the Olympics. How do you feel about this news?

R: It’s great news, we’ve been waiting for this announcement eagerly. I hope it will highlight video games and their players.

J: We hope so too, Rachel! To know more about you, what game do you play?

R: I’ve been playing League of Legends for 12 years now. I started playing when I was 12, with some friends from school who introduced me to the game.

J: Can you quickly explain the concept of League of Legends for our listeners?

R: Yes, of course. League of Legends is really the reference game in e-sports. Basically, you’re in a team of five players, each with a defined role. The games are played on an iconic map, the Summoner’s Rift, where you need to use strategy, pure skill, and, most importantly, coordination with your teammates to destroy the opposing Nexus.

M: You mentioned “pure skill.” Could you explain some specific terms and help our listeners?

R: So first, skill refers to the abilities I have when playing, like knowing how to help teammates.

M: In the FAQ, Bertrand asked about some unclear terms in League of Legends, like respawn, GL... Can you explain these terms? Or even GL.

R: Thanks, Bertrand, for your question. When I respawn with my main, I come back to life. And GL means Good Luck.

J: Regarding mains, we have Delila, a listener who asks what your favorite character is.

R: So, in League of Legends, we can choose from dozens of champions, each with their own abilities and style. It requires a lot of mastery because you need to manage your champion, react in real-time to your opponents' actions, and adjust your strategies based on the opposing team's movements while coordinating with your team. In the gaming world, we call the characters we mainly play "mains," and my favorite one to play is Ahri.

M: You talk about teamwork, how does it go for you in competitions? Do you play in a club?

R: Yes, exactly. I've been in a professional team for 4 years. My team is called “i rapaci.” We have 5 members.

J: You said you started professionally 4 years ago. Forgive me for asking, but did it feel risky to go pro in e-sports?

R: Yes, unfortunately. It was risky, but I always felt I wasn’t cut out for school. I’ve always been happier playing video games, and I finally took the leap in 2020. Of course, it was tough at first with my parents, who had trouble accepting it, but they saw how happy I was when they attended my first competition.

M: And for our last question, we have Michel, who asks how you see e-sports after the Olympics? How do you imagine the future of e-sports?

R: Mhh... Good question. It's certain that e-sports being recognized by a major institution like the Olympic Games will change a lot of things in this field. We hope it will be watched more and gain credibility worldwide. It’s something I really love, and I hope it will finally be appreciated for what it truly is after the Olympics.

J: Thanks for this interview, Rachel, and for opening up to us. We’re now going to move on to the second part, which is the game with 3 listeners.

M: 3 people have been randomly selected to answer a small quiz on e-sports. All the questions were covered in this interview, to win not 1 but 2 tickets to Los Angeles to see the events there.

Jingle

J: Let’s welcome Julie, André, and Thibault.

J: Hello Julie. You’re 22 years old and you live in Nice. What motivated you to play this game?

Julie: Hello, I’ve been playing video games for years as a hobby. Being in a situation of financial instability, I must say that a free trip to Los Angeles would be a great opportunity.

J: Thanks, Julie. Your question is: What is the name of the League of Legends map?

Julie: World maps?

J: Oh no, unfortunately, it’s wrong. The answer was “Summoner’s Rift”...

Julie: No worries, goodbye.

M: Now let’s move on to Thibault, who is 44 years old and lives in Strasbourg. Hello Thibault.

T: Hi team!!

M: So, Thibault, the question is: What does the term GL mean?

T: Aaaah, I don’t remember. I know “Good Game,” but GL... oh no, I can’t recall it. I’ll say Good Game anyway.

M: Unfortunately, that’s incorrect! Time’s up, you’ve lost. The answer was Good Luck!

T: Oh no, I knew it, but the radio stress got to me.

M: Yes, sometimes it can be tricky. Anyway, we wish you Good Luck in your future projects.

M: And now we move on to André, who is 72 years old and lives in Mauléon. Hello André.

A: Hello

M: So for this question, Rachel will ask you. But first, we’d like to know why you play?

A: I play because I’m really curious to see how video games will play out in the Olympics, and since there are 2 tickets to win, I’d love to take my grandson, who’s a huge fan of video games. He also plays League of Legends!

R: Hello André, thanks for participating. I’m going to ask you a question: What is my main/favorite character, André?

A: Ahri, I think.

R: That’s right! Congratulations, you win the tickets.

A: Oh great, my grandson will be so happy.

M: We wish you a great trip to LA.

R: Maybe you’ll come to the League of Legends events.

J: Thanks, André, and goodbye.

J: Finally, we’ve heard about a Netflix documentary series on e-sports.

M: Apparently, the series will follow several competitors in preparation for the 2028 Olympics. Are you part of it?

R: Well, this is the first time I’m giving this information, but yes, I’m part of it, no pun intended. There are three Italians being followed in their training, and I know there are also some French players involved.

M: Can you reveal their names for the French fans?

R: Ah, sorry, I’m not sure I’m allowed to share that. It’s a total exclusive! “Haha.”

J: Well, we tried. Let’s close the podcast on this loss.

M: Thank you, Rachel, and we wish you good luck for the Olympics!

R: Thank you, it was great.

M: And thanks to you for listening. We’ll see you next week for a podcast on our next player: Erick Santos aka Aspas in Valorant.
