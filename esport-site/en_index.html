The Rise of E-sports

You might think that e-sports is a recent invention, but it’s not! The first video games appeared in the 1950s, accompanied by competitions among enthusiasts on computers or consoles. Although simple at the time, these games laid the foundations for a global phenomenon. In the 1990s, everything took on a new dimension. Video games became mainstream, reaching a wide audience, while graphics and consoles improved in performance. The internet, becoming widely accessible, allowed players to compete remotely, paving the way for much larger competitions. These tournaments, initially local, evolved into international events bringing together players and spectators. Today, e-sports is a fully-fledged discipline, followed by millions of fans around the world. It’s no longer just a game, but a sporting and competitive experience.
