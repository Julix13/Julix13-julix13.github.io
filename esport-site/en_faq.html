Will e-sport events be accessible for free, or will a ticket be required?

A: Like other Olympic sports, access will require a ticket. However, some preliminary or demonstration events may be free to encourage participation.

Will e-sport events be available online for those who cannot attend in person?

A: Yes, all e-sport events will be streamed online via platforms like Twitch and official partner sites, ensuring global coverage.

Will specific infrastructures be set up for people with reduced mobility who wish to attend e-sport events?

A: Absolutely. The venues will comply with international accessibility standards, with ramps, elevators, and designated spaces for people with reduced mobility.

Will spectators have access to live commentary or explanations to understand the games?

A: Yes, each match will feature professional commentary, with explanations suitable for novice spectators. On-site screens will also display real-time infographics and statistics.

Will there be digital accessibility options, such as subtitles or real-time translations, for online streams?

A: Yes, subtitles and real-time translations will be available in multiple languages, both on streaming platforms and in viewing locations.

Will the venues hosting e-sport competitions comply with international accessibility standards?

A: Yes, all venues will comply with international standards, including provisions for disabled people, such as rest areas and accessible toilets.

How will deaf or hard-of-hearing spectators follow the competitions on-site?

A: Screens will display live subtitles for deaf or hard-of-hearing spectators, and sign language interpreters will be present in certain areas.

Will there be dedicated spaces for families or school groups to encourage the discovery of e-sport?

A: Yes, interactive spaces will be set up, featuring educational activities and e-sport introductions, specifically designed for families and school groups.

How do organizers plan to manage the potential crowd at popular events?

A: Organizers will implement a pre-booking system, limit venue capacities, and provide additional viewing areas to accommodate more spectators.

Will ticket prices for e-sport events be similar to those for other Olympic sports?

A: Ticket prices will be competitive and aligned with other sports disciplines.

Will spectators in remote areas have access to programs or initiatives to follow the competitions?

A: Yes, broadcast programs will be offered for free online, and local partnerships with television stations or community centers will help reach remote regions.

Will there be introductory sessions to help novice spectators understand the basics of competitive games?

A: Yes, online tutorials and on-site presentations will be available before the competitions to explain the basics of the games and how they are played.

Will e-sport events be organized in specific spaces, or integrated into traditional Olympic venues?

A: The events will be held in dedicated spaces equipped with state-of-the-art technology, while being located near traditional venues to encourage interaction with other sports.

Will local streaming platforms be used to expand access to events in under-connected countries?

A: Yes, partnerships will be established with local platforms and TV stations to ensure coverage in regions with limited connectivity, along with streaming on Twitch.

Will the events include interactive areas where spectators can try out the games?

A: Yes, interactive zones will be set up around the venues, allowing spectators to try out the featured games and interact with professional players.

How will digital accessibility be ensured for elderly people or those unfamiliar with technology?

A: Simple-to-use apps, tutorials, and on-site and online assistance points will be provided to guide novice users.

Will the competition schedules be adapted to the time zones of international spectators?

A: Yes, the schedules will consider major time zones. Replays and summaries will be available for everyone to follow the events.

Will dedicated mobile apps be created to provide real-time information and guides for attending events?

A: Yes, the Olympic app will allow users to track schedules, live scores, and provide interactive guides for an enhanced experience both on-site and remotely.
